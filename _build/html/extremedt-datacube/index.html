About the data¬∂

The following data cubes are available:

  • dtextreme_accumulated

  • dtextreme_height_levels

  • dtextreme_instantaneous

  • dtextreme_means

dtextreme_accumulated¬∂

Consists of five variables:

  • sf ‚Äì Snowfall

  • ssr ‚Äì Surface net short-wave (solar) radiation

  • ttr ‚Äì Top net long-wave (thermal) radiation

  • ro ‚Äì Runoff

  • tp ‚Äì Total precipitation

dtextreme_height_levels¬∂

Consists of two variables:

  • 100u ‚Äì 100 metre U wind component

  • 100v ‚Äì 100 metre V wind component

dtextreme_instantaneous¬∂

Consists of five variables:

  • sp ‚Äì Surface pressure

  • 10u ‚Äì 10 metre U wind component

  • 10v ‚Äì 10 metre V wind component

  • 2t ‚Äì 2 metre temperature

  • 2d ‚Äì 2 metre dewpoint temperature

dtextreme_means¬∂

Consists of one variable:

  • litota6 ‚Äì Averaged total lightning flash density over the last 6 hours

Temporal Resolution¬∂

The data are provided with a 1-hour time step.

File nameing convention:¬∂

dtextreme_<type_of_variable>_YYYYMMDD.nc YYYYMMDD - is the date when forecast starts (step 0)

DTExtreme at Hight Levels - data cube¬∂

Now, let‚Äôs try another data cube ‚Äî this time for wind at 100 m, using the horizontal wind components 100u and 100v.

In this example, we will generate maps for Japan:

  • a wind speed map,

  • a wind speed map with wind direction.

Wind speed formula¬∂

The wind components u and v form a horizontal wind vector.
The wind speed (vector magnitude) is computed as:

V = sqrt(u^2 + v^2)

where:

  • (u) is the zonal wind component (positive towards the east),

  • (v) is the meridional wind component (positive towards the north).
